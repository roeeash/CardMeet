— The status quo is broken

Selling cards online shouldn't feel like chasing ghosts.

PROBLEM 01
Coordinating in DMs requires both players to be online at once.

You message at 11pm. They reply at 8am. You're at work. They go to bed. By the time you're both online, the deal has cooled or someone else swooped in.

CardMeet's fix

Conventions you've already RSVP'd to become async coordination points. The app proposes the meetup; both sides confirm on their own time.

PROBLEM 02
Buyers ghost. Sellers are left holding cards.

You blocked off Saturday. They never showed. Did they back out? Forget? Find a better deal? You'll never know — and you sat at home with the cards instead of selling them to someone else.

CardMeet's fix

Both parties commit to a 30-min window. Miss it, and a no-show is recorded on your profile — visible to every future seller before they accept your offer.

PROBLEM 03
Bargaining in chat is a tangled mess.

"Wait, did you say ₪220 or ₪240?" Messages cross. Counter-offers get buried. Someone agrees to a price the other person already withdrew. Confusion ends in dropped sales.

CardMeet's fix

Structured offers replace chat. Each price is a discrete card with one action available — accept, counter, or withdraw. There's never doubt about whose turn it is.

— How it works

Four steps from "I want this card" to "I have it."

Set your reach

Pick how far you'll travel and which games you play. Conventions in radius auto-fill your calendar.

RSVP to events

Mark which conventions you'll attend. Your "Going" list becomes the matchmaking signal for every deal.

Offer or accept

Browse listings. Hit the asking price or send a structured counter-offer. No chat, no ambiguity.

Meet at the con

The app finds a convention you're both attending and locks in a 30-min window. Cash changes hands IRL.
